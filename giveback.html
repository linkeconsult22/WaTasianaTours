The Story Behind WaTasiana & Wasichana Wetu

My name is Marie Wambui. I was born and raised in Nairobi, Kenya. I left at 18 years of age in 1997 and now live in Los Angeles, California, raising my daughter Tasiana. My heart and mission remain deeply rooted in my home of Kenya.

My mom, Teresa Wanjiru Muigai, for years — and before she transitioned to heaven on April 21, 2024 — always reminded me and would say:

"Waa, please remember to pay it forward — remember to make a way to give to others." — Teresa Wanjiru Muigai

I've only ever witnessed my mom giving to others and paying the school tuition for families who otherwise could not afford to. My mom was a follower of God and a successful single mother of three daughters, who worked as a diplomat at the United Nations Nairobi, Kenya office for 36 years. Yet with all her responsibilities, mom chose to be of service to others. I grew up watching this behavior modeled. This was a norm for me — seeing kindness and consideration from my mom to others. That intentional message remained with me throughout my life.

After my mom transitioned, I learnt she was still educating a young girl whose family had been facing hardship. In May 2024, I felt honoured to take over what my late mom started and to continue to honor her legacy. I currently have four children in Nairobi who I feel blessed to educate.

How WaTasiana Was Born

In April 2025, while on our flight with Tasiana en route to my mom's one-year celebration of life in Nairobi, I wrote down a few prayer requests: "God please allow me to create a business where I can provide jobs for my fellow Kenyans." We returned home and in June 2025, with my background in the hospitality industry, I followed my passion to start a travel agency — yes, in Kenya, from Los Angeles.

Musa Lenkupae was our designated driver in Nairobi en route to the airport back to the US. I reached out to him with the idea and before we both knew it, we had assembled a remarkable team:

  • Musa Lenkupae – Director of Transportation & Lead Guide
  • Daniel Misuko – Head of Content Creation & Social Media Manager
  • Grace Wanjeri – Booking Agent & Data Protection Officer
  • John Kanjele – Executive Chef
  • Tasiana Wambui – Co-Founder & WaTasiana Traveling Ambassador
  • Marie Wambui – Owner, Founder & Lead Excursions Curator

I named the company after my daughter: Wa (short for our last name Wambui and the way my mom referred to me) and Tasiana, to honor my daughter. For Tasiana, her travel passion began at just two years old. Today, at thirteen, she has already immersed herself in the cultures of eleven countries spanning Africa, Europe, the Caribbean, North America, and South America — collectively, we have been to 17 countries.

The 48-Second Video That Changed Everything

In October 2025, a 48-second video revealed my true calling. That video was sent to me by Musa Lenkupae, who had received it from his cousin, Matthew Losieku, a young journalist in Wamba — a remote town in Samburu County, Kenya. Matthew came across it while preparing a proposal to provide sanitary pads for girls whose families could not afford them.

In the clip, a 10-year-old girl named Mpopi spoke with heartbreaking clarity. She expressed her desire to care for her body to avoid early pregnancy and pleaded for the chance to go to school.

Although Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) and child marriage are illegal in Kenya, these practices persist in remote areas, robbing girls of their childhoods, health, and futures. I asked Matthew to find Mpopi to ensure she was safe and in school. He first sought permission from her mother and father, then located her at a local school that taught only Samburu (the tribal language) and lacked essential resources. Mpopi welcomed Matthew's visit and made a simple, powerful request:

"Please find me a school where I can learn Kiswahili and English." — Mpopi, age 10, Wamba, Samburu County

Her plea opened the door. Within the months of October to December 2025, six more girls came forward — each fleeing FGM, forced marriage, or neglect. By the end of October 2025, Wasichana Wetu Rescue Charity was founded. 'Wasichana' means 'Daughters' or 'Girls' while 'Wetu' means 'Ours' — Our Daughters Rescue Charity.

The first four girls arrived with nothing but the clothes on their backs. We purchased bunk beds, mattresses, clothing, shoes, and all essentials. Our team has grown to include Chief Joel Lenkupae — a newly appointed head Chief in Wamba, Samburu East — who upholds the law and ensures anyone involved in illegal practices receives stern punishment, and Mama Rebecca Lenkupae, who opened her home as a safe house.

Our Model: From Rescue to Rise

We have built a careful, legally-sound system that ensures every girl's journey is one toward safety, education, and empowerment. We work formally with local chiefs, authorities, and children's offices — securing documentation and ensuring each girl receives healthcare and wellness checks.

Step 1

Rescue & Advocacy

Working formally with local chiefs and authorities to identify and legally remove girls from harmful situations.

Step 2

Holistic Care

A safe house in Samburu and comprehensive health support — girls arrive with nothing and receive everything they need.

Step 3

Quality Education

Full scholarships to Lentile Hills Academy, Nanyuki — a secure boarding school 600km from harm's reach.

Step 4

Community Impact

Initiatives that preserve Samburu culture while eliminating FGM, child marriage, and educational deprivation.
