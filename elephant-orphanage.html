About This Excursion

Embark on a profound and unforgettable journey into one of Nairobi's most inspiring wildlife sanctuaries — the Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage. This is not simply a sightseeing trip; it is an immersive conservation experience where you witness first-hand the remarkable work being done to rescue, rehabilitate, and reintroduce orphaned elephants back into the wild.

Founded in 1977 by Dame Daphne Sheldrick, the Trust is the world's most successful orphaned elephant rescue and rehabilitation program. Through groundbreaking research, Daphne Sheldrick pioneered the first successful elephant milk formula using coconut oil — a discovery that has saved hundreds of orphaned calves. The Elephant Orphanage serves as a nursery and transitional home, where young orphaned elephants receive round-the-clock care before being gradually reintroduced into protected wilderness areas.

The Trust has raised more than 270 orphaned elephants and successfully returned over 200 to the wild.
What You'll Experience

The Feeding Ritual

Watch baby orphaned elephants run excitedly towards their keepers for giant milk bottles — trumpeting and playfully jostling each other. Each orphan receives a specially formulated milk feed every three hours to support healthy growth.

Mud-Bath Playtime

After feeding, the elephants tumble joyfully into the mud bath. The mud helps cool their bodies, protects their skin from the sun, and acts as a natural insect repellent — pure joy to watch.

Hear Their Rescue Stories

Keepers share powerful stories about each elephant's rescue and recovery. Every orphan is named after the location where it was found. Meet their surrogate guardians — keepers who sleep beside the youngest calves in the stables every night.
